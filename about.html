The Artist

About Lindy

Lindy Ham is a South African artist whose work is deeply rooted in her love for the natural world. Born in the Eastern Cape, she spent years living and working in Johannesburg before returning to the life that had always called her back — life on a farm, surrounded by open skies, changing seasons, and the quiet beauty of the land.

That return changed everything. Immersed once again in the landscape, Lindy began translating the beauty around her into watercolour and oil paintings. What started as a personal expression of her connection with nature has grown into a body of work dedicated to capturing the essence of landscapes and the subtle emotions they evoke.

Artistic Style

Lindy specialises in watercolour and oil paintings, mediums that allow her to capture light, movement, and atmosphere with remarkable sensitivity. Her work is characterised by:

  • Soft, expressive brushwork that conveys emotion and movement
  • Harmonious colour palettes drawn from natural landscapes
  • Attention to light and shadow creating depth and atmosphere
  • A focus on landscapes and nature inspired by South Africa's beauty

The South African Influence

The South African landscape — with its vast horizons, diverse terrain, and changing light — has profoundly shaped Lindy's artistic vision. From her Eastern Cape beginnings to the farm she calls home today, this life has instilled in her a deep respect for nature and an eye for the subtle details others might miss: the way light catches water, the colour shifts in a sunset, the grace of wildlife in motion.

This unique perspective infuses every piece with authenticity and a genuine love for the natural world. Whether depicting serene coastal scenes or the vibrant movement of water and wildlife, Lindy's work invites viewers to pause, reflect, and reconnect with the beauty that surrounds us.

Available Works

Lindy's current collection features carefully selected watercolour pieces on paper. As her collection grows, each new work continues to explore themes of landscape, light, and the human connection to nature. With over 50 pieces created, a curated selection is being professionally photographed and made available for collectors and art enthusiasts.
