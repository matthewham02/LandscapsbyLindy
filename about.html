The Artist

About Lindy

Lindy Ham is a South African-born artist whose work is deeply rooted in her love for the natural world. Born in the Eastern Cape, she spent years living and working in Johannesburg before returning to a slower life on a farm, surrounded by open skies and changing seasons. Today she lives and paints in the rolling hillsides of England, where the quiet beauty of the countryside continues to shape every canvas.

Each move has added to her vision. Immersed in the landscape wherever she has called home, Lindy translates the beauty around her into watercolour and oil paintings — a body of work dedicated to capturing the essence of landscapes and the subtle emotions they evoke.

Artistic Style

Lindy specialises in watercolour and oil paintings, mediums that allow her to capture light, movement, and atmosphere with remarkable sensitivity. Her work is characterised by:

  • Soft, expressive brushwork that conveys emotion and movement
  • Harmonious colour palettes drawn from natural landscapes
  • Attention to light and shadow creating depth and atmosphere
  • A focus on landscapes and nature inspired by South Africa's beauty

The South African Influence

The South African landscape — with its vast horizons, diverse terrain, and changing light — profoundly shaped Lindy's artistic vision. From her Eastern Cape beginnings to the English hillside she now calls home, this life has instilled in her a deep respect for nature and an eye for the subtle details others might miss: the way light catches water, the colour shifts in a sunset, the grace of a flower in full bloom.

This unique perspective infuses every piece with authenticity and a genuine love for the natural world. Whether capturing a garden in full bloom or the quiet of a distant landscape, Lindy's work invites viewers to pause, reflect, and reconnect with the beauty that surrounds us.

Available Works

Lindy's current collection features original oil and watercolour paintings. As her collection grows, each new work continues to explore themes of landscape, light, and the human connection to nature. With over 50 pieces created, a curated selection is being professionally photographed and made available for collectors and art enthusiasts.
